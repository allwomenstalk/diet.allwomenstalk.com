# 7 Ways to Be a Part Time Vegetarian ...

**_Part-time vegetarianism_** is your golden ticket to a healthier lifestyle without giving up your love for a juicy steak now and then! Trust me, I’ve been a meat enthusiast for most of my life, but as life in 2024 rolls forward, I've discovered the joy and health benefits of embracing a more plant-centric diet. Balancing between different food choices can be a tasty journey - kind of like discovering that your third favorite pizza place actually has the best garlic knots.

The coolest thing about becoming a **part-time vegetarian** is the flexibility it offers. Imagine this scenario – one day you’re munching on a delicious quinoa salad with roasted veggies, the next you’re relishing your favorite chicken teriyaki. It’s like being a food chameleon, changing colors – or rather, flavors – to suit your mood and health goals. You can savor the best of both worlds and you don’t have to feel like you’re missing out on anything.

As you embark on this path, it’s essential to understand that you might face some friendly jabs from your friends or the odd look at family barbecues. Trust me, I've been the subject of jokes at more than one gathering. But once they taste your drool-worthy **vegetarian recipes**, there’s no doubt they'll be asking you for tips! One of the awesome aspects of this lifestyle change is the creative cooking it inspires. You might find yourself stepping up your chef game with _exclusive plant-based ingredients_.

Now, you might be curious about how to start and stay motivated. Fear not, because we've got some actionable steps. For instance, one way to ease into this is by [choosing specific days](#3) of the week to go meat-free. Calling it "Meatless Mondays" is a classic strategy that has helped many, including yours truly. Another approach is to explore [flexitarian meal plans](#5) that offer a perfect mix of veggie and occasional meat dishes.

The benefits aren't only health-related but extend to a positive environmental impact. By consuming less meat, you’re contributing to cleaner, greener earth, one meal at a time. Plus, those who have adopted a part-time vegetarian diet often report an increase in energy levels and better digestion.

So, if you're eager to try some [plant-based proteins](#4) or maybe curious about some [vegetarian dining hacks](#6), rest assured you’re heading in the right direction. Switching to a part-time vegetarian diet is like opening a new chapter in your food story – exciting, diverse, and full of delicious surprises!

## 1. Start Eating More Beans

For slow digesting complex carbs that will nourish your body cook beans in a soup or along with your veggies. Beans are a staple in the vegetarian diet because of the health benefits and also how well they fill you up!

## 2. Limit Your Meat

Limit your meat to one to two days a week and turn to other sources for protein like [nuts](https://food.allwomenstalk.com/varieties-of-nuts-you-should-be-eating-for-your-health/) and eggs. You will actually feel lighter and may even see the scale drop from this simple change. And as a part time vegetarian you can still [eat meat](https://food.allwomenstalk.com/perfectly-good-reasons-to-eat-meat/) one or two days and you will still stay on track to your health goals!

## 3. Stick to Whole Grains

Vegetarians eat [whole grains](https://food.allwomenstalk.com/tips-on-how-to-eat-more-whole-grains/) that aid their digestive tract, help them to feel better and provide energy to the body. So take a page from the book of this eating plan and eat healthy food that will help you get through each day with a spring in your step. Foods like brown rice and quinoa are the tops for whole grains!

## 4. Add Nuts

Add nuts like walnuts to your salad for your daily dose of Omega 3 and to help you eat a balanced diet. Vegetarians eat nuts in moderation because they are high in fat so eat no more than 1-2 handfuls per day!

## 5. And do Not Forget the Seeds

Pumpkin seeds are a nutrition powerhouse and can be just what you need when you finish your workout. Carry a bag of nuts and seeds for when you are on the road and stay true to eating healthier and being a part time vegetarian.

## 6. Eat Large Salads Every Day

To ensure you are eating enough veggies and staying on track to your part time vegetarian plan, make a salad with 3 cups of spinach with lots of veggies like mushrooms, peppers, beans and nuts. This bulky salad will fill you up without adding excess calories. Now that is a total win in the slim down department!

## 7. Be Creative with Vegetarian Recipe Creation

Simply type in vegetarian recipes for any search query and you will be amazed with all the results. I have made 6 bean soup along with many other great recipes by just searching online. You may find some new recipes that you will love in the process too! So be open to try something new and put your chef hat on to create!

So tell me are you ready to be a part time vegetarian? You may love the eating plan so much that you decide to become a full time vegetarian. Whatever you do, make sure you eat healthy, exercise and better your health!
