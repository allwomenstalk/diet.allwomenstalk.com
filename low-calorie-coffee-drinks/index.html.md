# 7 Low\-Calorie Coffee Drinks ...

**Low-calorie coffee drinks** – A topic near and dear to my heart. If there’s one thing that keeps me going through deadline-induced chaos, it has to be my beloved cup of coffee. Now, you might be wondering, how can coffee-essentially a black liquid with zero calories-be loaded with calories? Friends, it’s about those sneaky additions like sugar, syrups, and full-fat milk. I'm here to spill the beans (pun totally intended!) about some delicious, low-guilt options.

It’s 2024, and let’s be honest, the quest for a ***healthier lifestyle**_ has never been more crucial. Every corner café and chic restaurant is flaunting its range of _superfood salads_ and _smoothie bowls*, but let’s not forget one of the most enjoyable ways to watch the waistline: our precious coffee. Replace your calorie-laden favorites with some delightful, **low-calorie coffee alternatives** that won’t ruin your diet.

Picture this: strolling into your favorite local coffee shop, breathing in the aroma of freshly-ground coffee, and knowing exactly what to order that won’t add to your calorie count. You’ll feel a bit like a nutrition-savvy Sherlock Holmes!

For those of us attached at the hip to our morning brew, understanding different options like the light and lovely iced _Americano_, a dainty yet delectable _cappuccino made skinny_, or the oh-so-trendy _oat milk latte_, can truly transform our coffee consumption habits. And trust me, as someone who's tried every possible _low-sugar option_ on the menu, each of these alternatives offers a guilt-free indulgence.

In our article, we’ll delve into some solid reasons why shifting to low-calorie options is not just a diet-friendly move, but also a wallet saver. Not to mention, these beverages can be incredibly delicious when made right. In the section ["Why Choose Low-Calorie Coffee Drinks?"](#1), we explore compelling reasons that might surprise you. Plus, we’ll dish out ["Tips for Making Great Low-Calorie Coffee at Home"](#2) – because who doesn’t love a cozy coffee moment in their PJs?

Let’s make 2024 the year when we don’t just guzzle down energy-I mean, coffee-but do so with a touch of finesse and a big dose of _health consciousness_. So, get ready to discover some genuinely fantastic low-calorie coffee drinks that can keep you buzzing without the extra baggage. Cheers to being the caffeinated dynamo who’s also smart about calories! 📚🎉

## 1. Iced Spotted Owl

![Iced Spotted Owl](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/diet/2011/10/1_iced-spotted-owl_554x368.jpg)

**Calorie count:** 128
Wait, I thought we were supposed to be protecting the spotted owl, not drinking them! But they’re so very tasty! This latte is made with skim milk and sugar-free marshmallow, vanilla and hazelnut syrup. For an extra kick, try it with two shots of espresso… yow!

## 2. Pumpkin Spice Latte

![Pumpkin Spice Latte](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/diet/2011/10/2_pumpkin-spice-latte_500x333.jpg)

**Calorie count:** 148
This is my fall coffee drink favorite, either this way (low-cal) or with whipped cream on top (just a smidge!). It’s skim milk with one shot each of espresso and sugar-free pumpkin, gingerbread and cinnamon… so delicious!

## 3. Gingersnap Latte

![Gingersnap Latte](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/diet/2011/10/3_gingersnap-latte_400x400.jpg)

**Calorie count:** 198
This low-cal coffee drink is similar to the Pumpkin Spice latte, only instead of pumpkin and cinnamon, it’s just gingerbread with a shot of sugar-free marshmallow. It really does taste like a gingersnap cookie!

## 4. S’Mores Latte

![S’Mores Latte](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/diet/2011/10/4_smores-latte_560x372.jpg)

**Calorie count:** 200
While most of us consider s’mores to be a mid-summer camping thing, why not enjoy one as a coffee drink this fall? It’s a skim milk latte with sugar free shots of chocolate, marshmallow, and gingerbread. Hmm. Are you noticing a fall trend with all these gingerbread flavor shots?

## 5. Caramel Cocoa Latte

![Caramel Cocoa Latte](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/diet/2011/10/5_caramel-cocoa-latte_560x560.jpg)

**Calorie count:** 205
If you like the ooey-gooey flavor of caramel but want the coffee drink without the sticky after-effects, then try this! It’s (again) a skim milk latte with one shot each of sugar-free chocolate and caramel syrup. It has a completely different flavor than your typical mocha latte, or hot cocoa.

## 6. Chumpkin

![Chumpkin](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/diet/2011/10/6_chumpkin_500x337.jpg)

**Calorie count:** 227
I freely admit I wanted to include this low-cal coffee drink because of its name alone. But it actually does taste really, really good! It’s chai with pumpkin, which isn’t nearly as fun to say as “chumpkin.” Ask for it with skim milk, and without whipped cream, and maintain your diet without sacrificing sweets!

## 7. Black Coffee!

![Black Coffee!](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/diet/2011/10/7_black-coffee_500x333.jpg)

**Calorie count:** 5
Maybe you think all these fru-fru drinks are still a little on the higher-calorie end. Then why not have a plain old cup of rich black coffee? Too macho? Then keep in mind that skim milk adds 80 calories, and sugar adds 35, so feel free to doctor it up a bit.

I hate to say this out loud and all, but I am so excited for fall now that I’ve put together this list… all my favorite coffee drinks are on here! I can’t wait for my first Chumpkin of the season! Which of these delicious low-calorie coffee drinks is your favorite? Which one will you try first? Or is there another coffee drink recipe you’d like to share with me?
